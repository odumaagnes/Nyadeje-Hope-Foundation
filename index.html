About Us

A Legacy of Love & Service

My name is Agnes Oduma, I am a widow and a mother of 3 children, whom I had raised single-handedly and with a lot of challenges that prompted me to start Nyadeje Hope Foundation to support needy children and advocate for their rights in Siaya County.

My late mother Jane Aoko Okuom was called Nyadeje, a name given due to her place of birth according to Luo culture. Nyadeje lived her life helping needy children, especially orphans and the less privileged within her community. She ensured that no child brought to her attention missed school due to a lack of uniform or school fees. She sold illicit brew (changaa), cassava, and maize from her shamba just to ensure that a child would go to school. Her great love and devotion to serve children spurred me to work very hard in my life, which enabled me to join the social work field.

In 2023, she fell sick and later in the year 2024 she was diagnosed with cancer stage 4. While battling cancer, she was taking care of 4 of her step-sister's grandchildren after their mother died of HIV related complications. She breathed her last breath on 13/12/2025, and the children were left without anyone to support their education. Hence prompting me to stand in for them.

Having vast experience in the social work-related field which had motivated me to start this foundation, I have been working with vulnerable children for over 30 years, and my dream was to start a project that would transform the lives of children who have an inadequate support system that could hinder them from achieving their dreams and also become a voice to the voiceless.

My childhood was full of challenges, given that my parents had separated. I became vulnerable and hence subjecting myself to all forms of abuse, at a time I thought of committing suicide due to the trauma that I had but by the grace of God I survived the ordeal and today I am advocating for the rights of children.

"I survived what many children are going through today. That survival gave me a calling — to be the voice of the voiceless, to stand where no one else would stand, and to ensure that no child in Siaya County is left behind."

— Agnes Oduma, Founder

Our Story

The Boy at the School Gate

This foundation was established when I took my daughter to a new school called Kawuondi Primary School. At the gate, I found Joseph, a 7-year-old boy sitting outside.

Upon asking why he was not in school, he told me that his school fees had not been paid. He went home, but his mother insisted that he go back to school, without money. The boy was an orphan — his father died when he was 1 year old, and his mother had four other siblings that she was taking care of. Joseph had a torn uniform and shoes.

I eventually paid his fee, but I was not able to buy uniforms due to financial constraints I was facing as a result of losing my job in the month of December 2025.

I visited other primary schools around my village and found out that the problems affecting needy children were the same and most of them did not have good school uniforms, bags and shoes. Some children are walking barefoot while going to school because their guardians or parents cannot afford to buy shoes, and paying school fees is a problem too.

These have affected their learning in a negative way — sometimes they miss school, which has contributed to a great drop in academic performance. Those who have torn uniforms, especially girls, have had their self-esteem affected.

"My childhood was full of challenges. I became vulnerable and hence subjecting myself to all forms of abuse. At one time, I thought of committing suicide due to the trauma — but by the grace of God I survived. Today, I am advocating for the rights of children."

— Agnes Oduma, Founder

Our Objectives

Building a Brighter Future

These are the concrete goals that guide every effort of Nyadeje Hope Foundation — measurable, meaningful, and rooted in the real needs of the children we serve.

1

Enhance Smooth Learning for Needy Pupils

Enhancing smooth learning for at least 100 needy children yearly in primary schools by buying uniforms, shoes, and school bags, and paying fees for at least 20 students yearly in various secondary schools — removing the barriers that keep children out of the classroom.

2

Restore Dignity & Build Self-Esteem

Creating a conducive environment that enables a learner to have high self-esteem by ensuring that they dress well while in school — because every child deserves to feel confident, respected, and equal among their peers.

3

Empower Children to Know Their Rights

Empowering children to know their rights and to know the actions to be taken when their rights have been violated — equipping them with knowledge and courage to speak up and seek help when they need it most.

4

Nurture Talent Through Holiday Camps

Holding talent development camps during the holidays, where children can be taught and nurtured in different fields like art, music, technology, and more — helping them discover their God-given abilities beyond the classroom.

5

Engage & Educate Parents in the Community

Organizing parents' engagement forums in the community where parents will be trained and sensitized on good parenting, substance abuse, child abuse, and health talks — because transforming a child's life starts with strengthening the family around them.

Who We Help

Children Who Need Us Most

Orphans & Vulnerable Children

Children whose parents died from HIV-related complications or other causes, left under the care of aging grandparents or relatives, whose socioeconomic status makes them vulnerable and open to exploitation and abuse.

Children of Substance Abuse

Children coming from an imbalanced family where alcohol and drug abuse have taken control of their parents' lives and due to addiction, children are left without proper care.

Financially Unstable Families

Children who have both parents, but they are financially unstable due to their health condition or an inadequate source of income — unable to provide for basic needs and education.

Single-Parent Households

Children who are from single parents due to separation or divorce and the parent who is left is unable to support them adequately — leaving gaps in both emotional and financial support.

Children with Special Needs

Children with special needs, including those with various forms of conditions such as autism, physical disability, and other challenges that require additional support, resources, and understanding.

Abused or At-Risk Children

Children who are at risk of being abused or have been abused — needing protection, psychological support, safe spaces, and strong advocacy to ensure their rights are upheld.
